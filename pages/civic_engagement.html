My civic engagments

From a young age, I’ve been committed to making a positive impact in my community through various roles and projects. My civic journey began in middle school when I joined the Conseil Municipal des Jeunes in my city, where I served for three years. This opportunity allowed me to be involved in meaningful community initiatives, including the creation of the Baguette Suspendue project. This initiative, still ongoing today, encourages people to purchase extra baguettes at local bakeries. These baguettes are then collected and distributed by Les Restos du Cœur to people in need, providing fresh bread for those facing food insecurity.

My involvement continued through my role as a class representative during high school, my Classes Préparatoires, and my time in engineering school. Serving as a student representative allowed me to advocate for my peers, helping to foster a supportive and inclusive environment.

I am also proud to participate in Les Cordées de la Réussite, an initiative that supports students from underprivileged neighborhoods. By mentoring middle school students, I help them envision and plan their future projects, sharing skills and encouragement to inspire confidence in their potential.

In addition, I volunteer regularly with charities, participate in local maraudes to distribute essentials to homeless individuals, and have raised funds for initiatives like the Téléthon. Most recently, I joined the Octobre Rose run to raise awareness and support for breast cancer research.

I have volunteered at the Capitole du Libre, a major event dedicated to free software and hardware, too. I staffed in the Install Party room, where we assisted attendees with installing Linux systems and repairing their devices. Additionally, I helped out at the crêpe stand, contributing to the event’s friendly and welcoming atmosphere.

Through these experiences, I am continually inspired by the power of collective action and am committed to continuing my civic engagement, working towards a more inclusive and compassionate community.
